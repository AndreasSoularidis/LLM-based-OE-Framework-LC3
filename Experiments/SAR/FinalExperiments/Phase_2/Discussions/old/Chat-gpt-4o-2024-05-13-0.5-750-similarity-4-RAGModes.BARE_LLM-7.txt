### Iterative Discussion

#### Round 1: Initial Requirements Gathering

**Knowledge Engineer (KE):** 
Alright, team, we need to start by identifying the key concepts and relationships for our ontology. Let's start with the incidents and impacts. Domain Expert, what are the most important weather variables that can cause forest fires?

**Domain Expert (DE):** 
The most important weather variables are temperature, wind speed, and humidity. These factors significantly influence the likelihood and spread of wildfires.

**Knowledge Worker (KW):**
That makes sense. We also need to capture the current measurements and forecasts for these weather variables. Additionally, we should include the location of the incident and its priority during a forest fire disaster.

**KE:**
Great points. Let's also consider the urgency of incidents during forest fires. We need to create classes and properties to represent these aspects.

#### Round 2: Data from Human and Earth Observations

**KE:**
Next, let's discuss the data from human and earth observations. What kind of data should we include, Domain Expert?

**DE:**
We should include data from sensors, satellites, and social media. This data could provide information like the creation date, location, and classification of smoke. We also need to identify vulnerable objects involved in the incident and the status of wildfire forestry works.

**KW:**
Including data from various sources will help us monitor and assess the wildfire situation more effectively. We should create classes for sensors, satellites, social media, and their respective properties.

#### Round 3: Missions and Relationships Between Services

**KE:**
Finally, let's discuss the missions and relationships between services. What services or support do we offer for firefighting, Domain Expert?

**DE:**
We offer services like firefighting, evacuation, and medical aid. Each service is linked to specific missions, and we need to capture the location and urgency of these missions.

**KW:**
We should also include information about the population density and the location of involved people. This will help us coordinate and organize emergency response efforts more effectively.

**KE:**
Excellent. Let's start creating the ontology with these concepts and relationships in mind.
